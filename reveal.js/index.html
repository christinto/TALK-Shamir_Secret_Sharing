A straight line is described by the polynomial $f(x) = ax^2 + b$

First degree polynomial.

How many points are necessary to describe a line?

Two. Degree + 1.

Suppose we know two points: \[\begin{align} P1 &= (-16, -22) \\ P2 &= (-17, -26) \\ \end{align}\]

The two points represent our "shares" needed to recover the secret.

We can write the system of equations: \[\begin{align} f(-16) = -22 &= a(-16) + b \\ f(-17) = -26 &= a(-17) + b \\ \end{align}\]

We have two equations and two variables. We can find $a$ and $b$

There are other methods.

Not computationally efficient. LaGrange interpolation.

\[b = 16a - 22\]

Substite $b$ into the second equation.

Substituting $b$ into the second equation and simplifying \[\begin{align} -26 &= -17a + (16a - 22) \\ &= -a - 22 \\ a &= 26-22 \\ a &= 4 \\ \end{align}\]
Now that we know $a = 4$ we can find our secret, $b$ \[\begin{align} b &= 16a - 22 \\ b &= 16(4) - 22 \\ b &= 64 - 22 \\ b &= 42 \\ \end{align}\]
